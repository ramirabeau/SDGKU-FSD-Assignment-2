Biography

MASTER SERGEANT RAVEN R. MIRABEAU


He was born and raised in Baton Rouge, LA, and entered Basic Military Training at Lackland AFB in July 1995. Upon completion, he proceeded to Keesler AFB, MS, for technical training as a Ground Radio Communications Apprentice. He was then assigned to the 938th Engineering and Installations Squadron at McClellan AFB, CA in June 1996. After three years of installing various communications equipment and infrastructure, he was assigned to the 1st Combat Communications Squadron at Ramstein AB, Germany. After many deployments, he was assigned to the 72d Communications Squadron at Tinker AFB, OK, where he served as the base Personal Wireless Communications Manager. Two years later Sergeant Mirabeau was assigned to the 604th Air Support Operations Squadron in support of the 2d Infantry Division and United States Forces Korea at Camp Red Cloud, Republic of Korea. Leaving Korea, he was then assigned to 603d Air Control Squadron, at Aviano AB, IT. After the time in Italy, he headed back to Korea to serve as Noncommissioned Officer in Charge of the Distributed Ground Systems, Transmission Systems Section, Det 1, 694th Intelligence Surveillance, and Reconnaissance Group (ISRG), Osan AB, ROK. In 2009, MSgt Mirabeau was accepted for special duty as a Military Training Instructor (MTI) at JBSA-Lackland AFB. He graduated MTI School and was assigned to the 323d Training Squadron. In 2012 he transitioned over to the 319th TRS as an Instructor Supervisor and received his AETC Master Instructor Certification. Later in 2015, he headed back to Korea and the ISRG in the 694th Intelligence Support Squadron as Section Chief, Plans and Programs. In 2016, Sergeant Mirabeau was assigned to the 603d Air Communications Squadron, Ramstein AB, Germany as the Transmissions Systems Section Chief, later the Operations Flight Chief, and ultimately as Project Manager where he currently serves today. MSgt Mirabeau has been married to his wife Karen since 1995 and participates in various community ventures across the base and local community.
